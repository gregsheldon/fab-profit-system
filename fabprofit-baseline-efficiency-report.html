How FabProfit System benefits the business

1. Protects EBITDA before production

Margin leakage often becomes visible in the shop, but the economic decision was usually made earlier. FabProfit gives the company a structured way to identify scope, risk, labour, material, subcontractor, and quote-language exposure before award.

2. Standardizes quote governance

The system creates a repeatable path for scope breakdown, labour build-up, material costing, outsourced work, risk treatment, final price, and proposal language. That reduces variation between estimators and business units.

3. Increases useful estimating capacity

Faster estimating is not just speed. It gives leadership more time for review, customers faster responses, and estimators more time to catch the few issues that actually decide whether the job is worth winning.

4. Reduces key-person dependency

When the estimating process lives in a senior estimator's head, the business carries hidden continuity risk. FabProfit turns that judgment into tools, checklists, quote language, examples, and a repeatable company standard.

5. Improves quote-to-shop handoff

The quote should not die as a PDF in the customer's inbox. A strong estimate becomes the first production plan, with assumptions, exclusions, risk notes, material logic, and labour basis visible to the team that must build it.

6. Gives PE a post-close operating lever

For ownership groups, this is a practical value creation lever. It targets margin leakage, process variation, estimator onboarding, pricing discipline, and quote standardization without forcing a full ERP replacement.

90-day implementation path

Days 1 to 30: Diagnose and standardize

Map the current quote process, identify the highest-leak job types, set labour and material baselines, and define what must be checked before quote release.

Days 31 to 60: Install the quote workflow

Roll out the scope, material, labour, subcontractor, risk, pricing, and proposal workflow. Start using the system on selected live quotes and compare output to current process.

Days 61 to 90: Govern and calibrate

Create a quote review rhythm, capture actual-vs-estimate feedback, standardize quote language, and define the management dashboard for ongoing quote quality.

Highest-value fixes to prioritize

  1. Define a company-wide quote release checklist so pricing, risk, assumptions, exclusions, and scope boundaries are reviewed before submission.
  2. Separate shop labour, field labour, setup, movement, control time, friction time, fire watch, and site standby instead of hiding them inside one labour number.
  3. Standardize material buyout logic so the estimate reflects stock lengths, waste, drops, freight, coating, hardware, and price movement.
  4. Force a subcontractor and outsourced-work review that includes freight, prep, receiving, coordination, vendor risk, and your margin.
  5. Use a risk treatment method so uncertainty is priced, qualified, excluded, transferred, or reserved, rather than silently absorbed in profit.
  6. Turn every accepted quote into a shop handoff with assumptions, inclusions, exclusions, labour logic, material logic, and risk notes visible to operations.

Leadership discussion questions

Use these questions with ownership, the estimating manager, operations, purchasing, project management, and finance. The answers usually reveal the first places FabProfit should be installed.

Commercial and estimating

  • Which job types are we fastest at quoting, and which ones create the most rework?
  • Are we tracking quote revisions as a real cost?
  • Do all estimators use the same scope breakdown and quote review process?
  • Where do we most often get pressured into discounting without deleting scope?

Operations and margin

  • Where do jobs most often run out of hours?
  • Do PMs and foremen see the estimate assumptions after award?
  • Are material waste, finish, freight, install access, rentals, and risk visible before submission?
  • Do we know our true labour cost and margin before every quote leaves?
