What FabProfit is expected to change

These are realistic operational outcomes. They are not guaranteed financial results. They are the kinds of controls FabProfit is designed to install so leadership can see, price, qualify, and manage risk earlier.

1. Cleaner scope boundaries

The system pushes each job away from one lump sum and toward deliverables, inclusions, exclusions, assumptions, and controlled quote language.

2. More accurate labour logic

Labour is broken into build, review, setup, movement, control, friction, field event time, standby, and revision work instead of one optimistic hour number.

3. Better material buyout discipline

The report and system focus on what the job forces the shop to buy, including stock lengths, drops, waste, freight, coating moves, and supplier validity.

4. Outsourced work treated as managed scope

Vendor quotes are not treated as the full cost. FabProfit forces prep, freight, receiving, inspection, coordination, risk reserve, and margin into the review.

5. Risk named before award

The risk register approach gives each risk a cause, trigger, owner, cost, schedule impact, response, and quote treatment.

6. Final price tested before release

The final price is checked against complete cost, burdened labour, overhead, contingency, margin target, quote validity, and discount pressure.

7. Stronger quote trust

The quote becomes more than a number. It sells competence by showing scope, assumptions, exclusions, alternates, schedule, validity, and change rules.

8. Better learning loop

Estimate-vs-actual review turns closeout pain into updated standards, labour logic, risk notes, quote language, and future pricing discipline.

    90-day implementation path for your company

    Days 1 to 30: Diagnose and standardize

    Confirm the highest-leak job types, lock the quote-release checklist, review actual-vs-estimate data, and define the required scope/labour/material/risk checks.

    Days 31 to 60: Install the workflow

    Use FabProfit on selected live quotes. Force scope breakdown, labour buckets, material buyout logic, outsourced-work leveling, risk treatment, and quote language.

    Days 61 to 90: Govern and calibrate

    Review estimate vs actual, update standards, identify recurring misses, and turn the process into a repeatable company operating standard.

    Before the next 10 quotes leave the building

    This is the fastest practical sprint. It does not require rebuilding the whole company. It requires applying tighter quote discipline to the next live quoting cycle.

    1. Separate deliverables

    Break the job into actual deliverables before pricing one lump sum.

    2. Separate labour buckets

    Do not allow one blended labour guess to hide setup, movement, control, friction, field access, fire watch, or standby.

    3. Convert drawing quantity to buy quantity

    Confirm stock lengths, waste, drops, minimum orders, freight, coating moves, and supplier validity.

    4. Level outsourced quotes

    Carry prep, freight, receiving, inspection, admin, vendor risk, and margin, not just the vendor invoice.

    5. Name the top three risks

    For each risk, decide whether to price it, qualify it, exclude it, transfer it, reserve for it, or negotiate it.

    6. Create a shop handoff

    The accepted quote should give operations the assumptions and logic behind the number, not just a sell price.
